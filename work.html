Python frontend to MCNP(X)

An object-oriented way to generate and modify input files to codes such as Monte Carlo N-Particle Transport, a popular code used for the simulation of neutrons (and other subatomic particles) in systems such as power reactors and radiation detectors. Visit the project page at pyne.github.com.

Zorbit's Orbits 2D side-scroller game

A physics-based 2-D side-scroller game, written in C++, using the Simple and Fast Multimedia Library (SFML) and the open source Box2D physics engine. A unique feature of the game is that the levels are circular. The website for the game is here.

Introductory engineering course

Neutronics model

An implementation of a computational model for the flow of neutrons in a power reactor whose geomoetry is modeled as multiple homogeneous regions arranged in an infinite repeating lattice. This was an MS thesis project at the University of Texas at Austin under Dr. Deinert. Contact me to obtain the thesis.

Fuel cladding hybridization

Flash stick figure animation

A flash animation created for the 2011 graduating class of the Sibley School of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Cornell University. The video shows the journey of a student through his/her required classes, and each class comes to life and poses a challenge to the student.

Legged locomotion manuscript

A manuscript written with Alan Argondizza based off course notes for the Legged Locomotion course taught at Cornell by Dr. Ruina. Topics include fundamentals about muscles and motors, experimental work and theory on energy usage, the SLIP (inverted pendulum) model, and control.
