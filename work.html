Python frontend to MCNP(X)

An object-oriented way to generate and modify input files to codes such as Monte Carlo N-Particle Transport, a popular code used for the simulation of neutrons (and other subatomic particles) in systems such as power reactors and radiation detectors. Visit the project page at pyne.github.com.

Zorbit's Orbits 2D side-scroller game

A physics-based 2-D side-scroller game, written in C++, using the Simple and Fast Multimedia Library (SFML) and the open source Box2D physics engine. A unique feature of the game is that the levels are circular. The website for the game is zorbitsorbits.com.

Introductory engineering course

A 10-week course designed from my undergraduate experience in mechanical engineering. It was taught at the Phillips Academy boarding school to five students, all much smarter than me. We covered programming (in MATLAB), statics, finite element analysis of trusses, dynamics, "robotics", and system dynamics, and the course finished with a design project. The website for the course is http://sites.google.com/site/paengr.

Neutronics model

An implementation of a computational model for the flow of neutrons in a power reactor whose geomoetry is modeled as multiple homogeneous regions arranged in an infinite repeating lattice. This was an MS thesis project at the University of Texas at Austin under Dr. Deinert. Contact me to obtain the thesis.

Fuel cladding hybridization

Experimental research performed as part of The Pennsylvania State University's Toshiba-Westinghouse Fellowship Program. The hydridng of zirconium fuel rods, caused by the flow of water around the fuel rods, embrittles the fuel rod through the formation of zirconium hydride platelets. My work was to design, through experiment, the experimental procedure that would be used in more substantial experiments. Here is a poster on this work presented at the 2010 American Nuclear Society meeting.

Flash stick figure animation

A flash animation created for the 2011 graduating class of the Sibley School of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Cornell University. The video shows the journey of a student through his/her required classes, and each class comes to life and poses a challenge to the student. See related videos, created for the same project, at Anthony Chen's vimeo page.

Legged locomotion manuscript

A book manuscript written with Alan Argondizza based off course notes for the Legged Locomotion course taught at Cornell by Dr. Ruina. Topics include fundamentals about muscles and motors, experimental work and theory on energy usage, the SLIP (inverted pendulum) model, and passive control. Contact any of us for more information.

Rigid body simulation using Featherstone's algorithm

The beginnings of a three dimensional rigid body dynamics engine, based on the articulated body algorithm formulated by Dr. Featherstone. It is mostly an object-oriented port to C++ (using the Gnu Scientific Library) of MATLAB code that Dr. Featherstone has on his website, with my own simple path planning and control. This is something that I would really enjoy continuing to work on.

Image analysis for low-gravity droplet combustion experiments

Some researchers are studying hydrocarbon fuels via low-gravity droplet combustion experiments in which the evolution of a droplet's diameter (initially ~1 mm) in time is recorded while the droplet is in free fall (about 1 second). In this project, we automated the analysis (droplet diameter measurement) of the 100 images that result from such an experiment. A paper on this topic has been accepted to the journal Image Analysis and Stereology.

Electrical work for Cornell University Solar Decathlon

Every two years, the Department of Energy selects 20 universities to design and build an 800 square foot solar house, and has them bring them to the National Mall in D.C. (in 2013, they'll be in California) to show to the public. For Cornell's 2009 team, I performed electrical design, performed most of the electrical wiring of the house, and some of the installation of the house's solar panel system.
